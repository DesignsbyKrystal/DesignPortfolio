Garden Tool Set

Our design challenge was to create a garden tool set with no flat surfaces, blending three distinct themes. The philosophy behind this project was “form over function,” prioritizing ergonomics, flow, and user-centered design over aesthetic convention.

Design Philosophy

My design process focused on user-centered solutions rather than purely aesthetic choices. Every curve and surface was tailored to the way a user holds and manipulates the tools, balancing comfort, grip, and accessibility.

Physical Modeling Experience

I brought the top concept to life using foam and shop machinery, including a bandsaw and sander. This hands-on experience allowed me to understand form, weight, and ergonomics in a tangible way, further informing the final design.

CAD Modeling & Rendering

I approached CAD modeling and rendering as a beginner and quickly advanced to an intermediate level. Each tool was iteratively modeled, refined, and rendered to test both visual appeal and ergonomic performance.

Final Design

The final tool set embodies a seamless flow of forms, with no flat surfaces and intuitive contours. Every element was designed to serve the user, creating a functional yet visually striking set of garden tools.
