One of My First Physical Product Designs

Our design challenge was to create a perfectly round, horizontally revolved set of glasses—one 16oz and one 6oz. This project transcended functional glassware to become a statement of refined taste. Guided by a design philosophy that emphasizes form, balance, and user experience, I focused on elegant sphere and cone shapes, an hourglass profile, and smooth, rounded bases. Ergonomics were a key consideration, ensuring a natural, comfortable grip while maintaining thoughtfully composed negative space. The result is contemporary, luxury glassware that harmonizes aesthetic appeal with functional design.

Sketch Ideation

I created multiple sketches totaling about 24 concepts. Here are the 3 final concepts that made it to refinement.

Flower Concept

The flower concept changed significantly from the last version. I aimed to create a more realistic form incorporating a petal-like rim and used the same petal feature between the stem and cup of the smaller glass.

Layered Glass Concept

This concept focused on the feel in the user's hand. I moved the exterior layers on the bigger glass to the top for better grip and refined the inner layering design for the smaller glass.

Final Choice: Bow Tie Glass Set

Ultimately, I chose the Bow Tie glass set. I selected this set because of its dynamic transitions, perfect balance, functionality, and bold visual identity. It represents the culmination of ergonomic refinement, visual appeal, and tactile comfort.
